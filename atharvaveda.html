अथर्ववेद
Atharva Veda

ॐ

The Atharva Veda is the fourth Veda, named after the sage Atharvan who is credited with discovering fire and establishing its sacred rituals. Unlike the other three Vedas which focus primarily on ritual worship, the Atharva Veda addresses the practical concerns of daily life — healing, protection, prosperity, and longevity.

Often called the "Veda of the People," it contains 730 hymns with approximately 6,000 verses covering medicine (the foundation of Ayurveda), philosophy, cosmology, and prayers for various life situations. The Atharva Veda also includes some of the most profound philosophical hymns, including early explorations of Brahman and the nature of reality.

"The earth is my mother, and I am her child.
May she grant me wisdom and nourishment."
— Atharva Veda 12.1

Prithvi Sukta - Hymn to Mother Earth

Atharva Veda 12.1.1 — One of the world's oldest ecological hymns

सत्यं बृहद्ऋतमुग्रं दीक्षा तपो ब्रह्म यज्ञः पृथिवीं धारयन्ति ।
सा नो भूतस्य भव्यस्य पत्न्युरुं लोकं पृथिवी नः कृणोतु ॥
Transliteration:
Satyaṃ bṛhadṛtamugram dīkṣā tapo brahma yajñaḥ pṛthivīṃ dhārayanti
Sā no bhūtasya bhavyasya patnyuruṃ lokaṃ pṛthivī naḥ kṛṇotu

Translation:
Truth, cosmic order, consecration, austerity, prayer and sacrifice sustain the Earth.
May she, the mistress of what has been and what will be, make a wide world for us.

Commentary

The Prithvi Sukta (Bhumi Sukta) is a remarkable hymn of 63 verses dedicated to Mother Earth. Composed over 3,000 years ago, it expresses profound ecological awareness and reverence for the natural world that resonates powerfully today.

Satyam (Truth) — The foundation that upholds all existence
Ritam (Cosmic Order) — The natural law that governs the universe
Tapas (Austerity) — Spiritual discipline and self-restraint
Brahma & Yajna — Sacred knowledge and sacrificial offerings

This hymn teaches that the Earth is sustained not merely by physical forces but by moral and spiritual principles. Human righteousness directly affects the well-being of the planet — a teaching that anticipates modern environmental ethics.

Healing Prayer

Atharva Veda 2.29.7 — Prayer for restoration of health

यदि क्षितायुर्यदि वा परेतो
यदि मृत्योरन्तिकं नीत एव ।
तमा हरामि निरृतेरुपस्थाद्
अस्पार्षमेनं शतशारदाय ॥
Transliteration:
Yadi kṣitāyuryadi vā pareto
Yadi mṛtyorantikaṃ nīta eva
Tamā harāmi nirṛterupasthād
Aspārṣamenaṃ śataśāradāya

Translation:
Whether his life has been exhausted, or whether he has departed,
Or whether he has been brought to the vicinity of death,
I bring him back from the lap of destruction,
I have rescued him for a hundred autumns (years) of life.

Commentary

This powerful healing mantra reflects the Atharva Veda's role as the foundation of Ayurveda (the science of life). Ancient Vedic healers combined herbal medicine with mantras, understanding that healing occurs on physical, mental, and spiritual levels simultaneously.

Nirrti — The goddess of decay and destruction, representing the forces that lead to death and dissolution
Shata-sharada — "Hundred autumns," the Vedic ideal of a full lifespan of 100 years

The Atharva Veda contains numerous such healing prayers, establishing the principle that spiritual intervention can restore health even in dire circumstances. This holistic approach continues in Ayurvedic medicine today.

Shanti Sukta - Prayer for Universal Peace

Atharva Veda 19.9.14 — Invocation for peace in all realms

द्यौः शान्तिरन्तरिक्षं शान्तिः
पृथिवी शान्तिरापः शान्तिरोषधयः शान्तिः ।
वनस्पतयः शान्तिर्विश्वे देवाः शान्तिर्ब्रह्म शान्तिः
सर्वं शान्तिः शान्तिरेव शान्तिः सा मा शान्तिरेधि ॥
Transliteration:
Dyauḥ śāntirantarikṣaṃ śāntiḥ
Pṛthivī śāntirāpaḥ śāntiroṣadhayaḥ śāntiḥ
Vanaspatayaḥ śāntirviśve devāḥ śāntirbrahma śāntiḥ
Sarvaṃ śāntiḥ śāntireva śāntiḥ sā mā śāntiredhi

Translation:
May there be peace in the heavens, peace in the atmosphere.
May there be peace on earth, peace in the waters, peace in the plants.
May there be peace in the trees, peace among all the gods, peace in Brahman.
May everything be peaceful, peace alone, peace. May that peace come to me.

Commentary

This magnificent Shanti Sukta envisions peace radiating through every level of existence — from the celestial realms to the earth, from the waters to the plants, from all beings to the ultimate reality (Brahman).

The prayer recognizes that true peace cannot exist in isolation. Peace must pervade:

Cosmic level — Dyaus (sky/heaven) and Antariksha (atmosphere)
Natural level — Earth, waters, plants, and trees
Divine level — All the gods and Brahman itself
Personal level — "May that peace come to me"

This holistic vision of peace — extending from the cosmic to the personal — represents one of humanity's earliest and most beautiful prayers for universal harmony.
