यजुर्वेद
Yajur Veda

ॐ

The Yajur Veda is the Veda of prose mantras used in ritual worship and sacrificial ceremonies (yajnas). The name derives from "yajus" meaning "worship" or "sacrifice" and "veda" meaning "knowledge." It provides the practical formulas and instructions for performing Vedic rituals.

The Yajur Veda exists in two main recensions: the Krishna (Black) Yajur Veda, which mixes mantras with prose explanations, and the Shukla (White) Yajur Veda, which separates mantras from their commentaries. Both traditions preserve essential knowledge for connecting the human and divine realms through sacred ritual.

"May there be peace in the heavens, peace on earth, peace in the waters,
peace in the plants, and peace in all beings."
— Yajur Veda

Shanti Mantra

Taittiriya Upanishad 2.2.2 — Prayer for harmony between teacher and student

ॐ सह नाववतु ।
सह नौ भुनक्तु ।
सह वीर्यं करवावहै ।
तेजस्वि नावधीतमस्तु मा विद्विषावहै ।
ॐ शान्तिः शान्तिः शान्तिः ॥
Transliteration:
Om Saha Nāvavatu
Saha Nau Bhunaktu
Saha Vīryaṃ Karavāvahai
Tejasvi Nāvadhītamastu Mā Vidviṣāvahai
Om Śāntiḥ Śāntiḥ Śāntiḥ

Word-by-Word:
ॐ (Om) — the sacred syllable, the divine sound
सह (Saha) — together, with
नौ (Nau) — us both, the two of us (dual)
अवतु (Avatu) — may [he] protect (3rd person imperative)
भुनक्तु (Bhunaktu) — may [he] nourish, may [he] sustain (3rd person imperative)
वीर्यम् (Vīryam) — vigor, energy, strength
करवावहै (Karavāvahai) — may we both make, may we do together (1st person dual)
तेजस्वि (Tejasvi) — brilliant, radiant, full of splendor
अधीतम् (Adhītam) — what is studied, learning
अस्तु (Astu) — may it be (3rd person imperative)
मा (Mā) — not, never (prohibitive particle)
विद्विषावहै (Vidviṣāvahai) — may we hate each other (1st person dual)
शान्तिः (Śāntiḥ) — peace (repeated three times for the three planes)

Translation:
Om, May we both (teacher and student) be protected together.
May we both be nourished together.
May we work together with great energy.
May our study be enlightening and fruitful.
May we never have hostility towards each other.
Om, Peace, Peace, Peace.

Commentary

This beloved Shanti Mantra from the Taittiriya Upanishad is traditionally chanted at the beginning of spiritual study sessions. It invokes blessings for the sacred relationship between guru (teacher) and shishya (student).

Saha Nāvavatu — "May we both be protected" — Acknowledges that both teacher and student need divine protection on the journey of knowledge
Saha Nau Bhunaktu — "May we both be nourished" — True learning nourishes both parties; the teacher grows by teaching
Saha Vīryaṃ — "Together with vigor" — Learning requires energy and enthusiasm from both sides
Mā Vidviṣāvahai — "May we not hate" — The bond must remain free from ego conflicts and misunderstandings

The threefold repetition of "Śāntiḥ" (peace) addresses the three sources of obstacles: Ādhidaivika (cosmic/divine), Ādhibhautika (physical/environmental), and Ādhyātmika (internal/mental). This mantra reminds us that true education is a collaborative journey requiring mutual respect, dedication, and harmony.
