Sukta Collections

ॐ

A Sukta (Sanskrit: सूक्त, "well-spoken") is a hymn composed of multiple verses (mantras) dedicated to a particular deity, concept, or purpose. Each Sukta forms a complete unit of devotional or philosophical expression within the Vedas.

This section organizes mantras by their Sukta collections, allowing you to explore complete hymns and understand the context in which individual mantras appear. Browse the collections below to discover the rich tapestry of Vedic wisdom.

Available Collections

गायत्री मन्त्र

Gayatri Mantra

The most sacred mantra of the Vedas, a prayer to Savitr for illumination of the intellect. From Rig Veda 3.62.10.

पुरुष सूक्त

Purusha Sukta

The Hymn of the Cosmic Being, describing the creation of the universe through divine sacrifice. From Rig Veda 10.90.

गणेश सूक्तम्

Ganesha Suktam

Hymns to Lord Ganesha, the remover of obstacles and lord of beginnings. From Rig Veda 8.81.

शान्ति मन्त्र

Shanti Mantra

Prayer for harmony between teacher and student. From Taittiriya Upanishad 2.2.2.

पृथिवी सूक्त

Prithvi Sukta

One of the world's oldest ecological hymns, dedicated to Mother Earth. From Atharva Veda 12.1.

उद्गीथ

Udgitha

The meditation on Om as the essence of all sacred sound. From Chandogya Upanishad 1.1.1.
